Saturday sport: Cork squeeze past Roscommon; Dublin trail Clare at half-time<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Here’s the latest from the day’s sporting action…<\/p>\n\n\n\n

GAA<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

The first of the All-Ireland Senior Football Championship preliminary quarter-finals have got underway.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

A late point for Cork at P\u00e1irc Ui Chaoimh has seen them past Roscommon on a scoreline of 1-14 to 0-16 to take a place in the All-Ireland Football quarter-finals.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The second of the day’s preliminary quarter-finals between Kildare and Monaghan threw in at 4.45pm, while Ballybofey hosts Donegal v Tyrone from 7pm.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In the All-Ireland Hurling Championship, Dublin have built up a heavy deficit against Clare in the first of two quarter-finals at the Gaelic Grounds. The Banner lead 4-8 to 1-8 at half-time.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Later, Galway take on Tipperary from 6.15pm, with the winners of the two games meeting Limerick and Kilkenny in the semi-finals.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In Group Two of the All-Ireland Senior Camogie Championship, Kilkenny took a 4-13 to 1-4 win over Dublin at Parnell Park, while Tipperary lead Wexford 2-10 to 0-10 with just a few minutes gone in the second half in Bellefield.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Rugby<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

Ireland have drawn 34-34 against England in their opening game at the under-20 World Rugby Championship in South Africa.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Next up for Ireland is Australia on Thursday at 10am Irish time.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Golf<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

Leona Maguire leads the way heading into the third round at the Women’s PGA Championship in New Jersey.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Chasing a first major title, the Cavan woman is one shot clear of the field on five-under-par.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Stephanie Meadow is also well-placed on one-under.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Elsewhere, Rory McIlroy is seven shots off the lead ahead of his third round at the Travelers Championship.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

He’s eight-under-par – with Denny McCarthy and Keegan Bradley at the head of the field on 15-under.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
