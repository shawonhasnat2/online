It has been almost a week now since the incident involving Miami Dolphins wide receiver Tyreek Hill at a North Miami Beach marina, and there remains a lot that is unknown.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

What we do know is that the investigation by Miami-Dade Police remained open as of Friday, according to UPI.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Details of the incident remain as murky as ever, though, with The Miami Herald <\/em>and South Florida ABC affiliate WPLG each reporting that tarpon fishing from the dock, which is not allowed, played a role in the dispute and the police report indicating that what led to Hill allegedly slapping a man in the back of the head was two women stepping onto a boat and being asked to leave.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Hill and his agent, Drew Rosenhaus, both posted videos of their fishing outing on Father’s Day that included Rosenhaus jumping into the ocean to touch a shark near the boat, but neither has commented on the incident.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

THE DAVANTE ADAMS SITUATION<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

A report in the\u00a0Kansas City\u00a0<\/em>Star on Friday indicated that criminal charges were dropped against Raiders star wide receiver Davante Adams,\u00a0charges stemming from an incident after a Monday night game at Arrowhead Stadium in October when Adams was seen on video shoving a man carrying photo\/video equipment after he walked in front of him.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Adams still is facing a civil lawsuit from the victim, and Pro Football Talk suggested Saturday morning that Adams now could be facing disciplinary action from the NFL through its Code of Conduct policy.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

This, of course, relates to Hill in a couple of ways, the first being how long it took for that particular assault case to be resolved, and the second being the potential of an NFL investigation.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

WPLG reporter Ian Margol said an NFL representative was at the Haulover Marina on Friday.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

THE HILL INCIDENT POLICE REPORT<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

The\u00a0South Florida Sun-Sentinel first\u00a0<\/em>revealed the contents of the police report of the incident,\u00a0which alleged that Hill hit a man in the neck and later charged at him before being restrained by members of his group.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

On Wednesday, before the Sun-Sentinel report, South Florida radio host Andy Slater, citing sources, reported that police had closed the matter and the alleged victim would have to go to the state attorney’s office if he wished to press criminal charges.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

According to the police report, the incident involving Hill began when two women got on a charter fishing boat without permission and were asked to leave. After an argument, the unidentified \u201csubject\u201d (believed to be Hill) slapped one of the employees on his neck with an open hand while he was being removed from the argument and ran toward the victim, but several people restrained him.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The subject then fled the area, according to the report.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The Dolphins issued a statement regarding the situation, and it pretty much was what one should expect in circumstances like these.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u201cWe are aware of the situation and have been in contact with Tyreek, his representatives and the NFL,” the statement read. “We will reserve further comment at this time.\u201d<\/p>\n\n\n\n

WHAT HAPPENS NEXT?<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

With the Dolphins done with the offseason program and the start of training camp more than a month away, this is the time of year when no news is good news when it comes to players.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The Dolphins no doubt will continue to monitor the situation, as will the NFL and the league’s Code of Conduct could come into play depending on how things play out.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Hill is heading into his second season with Miami after a first one that met \u2014 if not exceeded \u2014 all expectations the team could have had when it sent five draft picks to the Kansas City Chiefs to consummate the trade.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Not only did Hill earn team MVP and All-Pro honors, he was a tone-setter with his practice habits and confident approach from the start of the offseason program.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

He’s looking for bigger and better things in 2023, saying after the final practice of minicamp his goal was to reach 2,000 receiving yards.<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

The weekend incident, though, brought back earlier off-the-field incidents that threatened to derail his career \u2014 he was dismissed from Oklahoma State after being charged with domestic abuse (he later pleaded guilty) and was investigated in 2019 for alleged battery, though the case was dropped after medical reports indicated his 3-year-old son’s arm was broken bracing for a fall. The NFL investigated Hill but ultimately did not suspend him after concluding he had not violated the league’s code of conduct policy.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

After the trade with the Chiefs, Dolphins GM Chris Grier was asked about the kind of background work that was done regarding Hill.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u201cGoing forward, we feel good about it,” Grier said. “We talked to a number of people that have been around Tyreek, especially in Kansas City the last few years. For us, we\u2019re comfortable moving forward and the expectation, like him, will be for every player on the roster \u2014 it\u2019s to be a good teammate and a good citizen in South Florida.\u201d<\/p>\n\n\n\n
